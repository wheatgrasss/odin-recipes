Oyster Steak Recipe

    Ingredients
  • 2 oyster steaks
  • 1 tbsp high-heat cooking oil (like olive or avocado)
  • 1 tbsp butter
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • Sprigs of fresh rosemary and thyme
  • 1/4 cup beef stock or broth
  • Salt and freshly ground black pepper to taste
  1. Prepare the steaks. Pat the oyster steaks completely
    dry with a paper towel. Season both sides generously with salt and pepper.

  2. Sear the steaks. Place the steaks in the hot pan and
    sear for 1–2 minutes per side for a thin cut, or 3–4 minutes for a
    thicker steak, until a nice crust forms.
  3. Sear the steaks. Place the steaks in the hot pan and
    sear for 1–2 minutes per side for a thin cut, or 3–4 minutes for a
    thicker steak, until a nice crust forms.

  4. Add aromatics. When you flip the steaks for the final
    sear, add the butter, minced garlic, rosemary, and thyme to the pan.
    Baste the steaks with the melted butter and herb mixture.

  5. Finish cooking. For medium-rare doneness, cook the
    steaks until the internal temperature reaches 135°F, using a meat thermometer.

  6. Rest the steaks. Transfer the steaks to a clean plate
    and tent them loosely with foil to rest for 10 minutes. This allows the
    juices to redistribute for a tender, juicy result.

  7. Create the pan sauce. While the steaks are resting, add
    the beef stock to the hot pan and scrape up any browned bits from the bottom.
    Continue to simmer until the sauce has reduced slightly.

  8. Serve. Slice the rested steak thinly against the grain and
    pour the pan sauce over the top.
